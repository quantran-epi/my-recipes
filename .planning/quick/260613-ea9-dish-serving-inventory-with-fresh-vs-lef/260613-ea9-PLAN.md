<objective>
Add a "dish serving inventory" that separates FRESH cooked servings from existing LEFTOVER servings while reusing the existing `LeftoverTrackerItem` model and store. Finishing a cooking session produces fresh servings; completing a meal consumes servings; when both kinds exist for a dish the user selects which to use.

Purpose: Track how many ready-to-eat servings of a dish exist (fresh vs leftover) so meal completion can draw down the right stock, without inventing a parallel persist slice.
Output: A `kind: 'fresh' | 'leftover'` discriminator on `LeftoverTrackerItem`, a `consumeDishServings` reducer, a grouping selector, fresh-serving production at cook finish, a kind-aware consumption step at meal completion, and a management screen that visually distinguishes the two kinds.
</objective>

<design_notes>
Tradeoff (decided): Extend the existing `LeftoverTrackerItem` model with a `kind` discriminator rather than adding a new persist slice. Reasons: (1) fresh and leftover servings share identical fields (dishId, portions, eatBy, status, meal linkage), selectors, the eat/finish/discard actions, and the management UI; (2) the slice already auto-persists under `personal.appContext`; (3) a new slice would duplicate all four reducers and the management screen. Cost: existing records lack `kind` ‚Äî handled by treating absent `kind` as `'leftover'` everywhere (a `normalizeKind` helper), so persisted state needs no migration.

Serving math: produced servings == `session.targetServings` (the count the session was scaled to). At meal completion, the consumed count per dish defaults to `dishServings?.[dishId]` when available, else the dish base servings; it is editable and capped at the available stock of the chosen kind.
</design_notes>
